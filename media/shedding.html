shedding.txt

She sang swan songs to children
With less than ten feathers, their flock hanging by threads.
She gave them fuel for the blazes in their hearts, of rebellion,
Of dreaming, hurting, fighting
Fractured homes.
Never alone, you could see graves in her thoughts,
Resting souls wilted from years gone by.
She could feel her husk shedding as she gave thick skin to young children
and her fears became childish,
and her weakness evaporated,
and her tongue became gold from silver,
and her pen spouted words of juvenile angst and elderly wisdom.
To this day, she sheds still.
